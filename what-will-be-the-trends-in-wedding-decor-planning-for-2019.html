What Will Be The Trends In Wedding Decor Planning For 2019

The wedding is probably the most significant event of anyone’s life and if you and your partner have planned to get married in 2019, it not only has the contemporary styles with a timeless touch but also has a lot of modern and trendy to consider. So, if you are wondering about the best wedding decorations to choose a unique style for your big day, here we bring you the list of latest trends in wedding event decorations to make your wedding day more special.

The first and the most common see to sight this year would be customized bars. The trendy modern laser cut will bring life to the bar designing for your event with traditional clematis all around the edges of the table. They will not only transform the experience of the bar but will also help to add a unique styling factor to your event.

If you are planning an outdoor wedding which takes you closer to nature, another site which can make your wedding day more special is the English style of garden set up. Silks could be used to wrap the flowers pots to add a little charm to the entire decor arrangement. The set up will connect with the environment at the same giving a distinguished look to the garden set up.

If you are planning for a wedding event that will be hosted entirely in an indoor venue, then the Wedding Flowers would be seen covering the large size banquet halls which would be as big as the museums or loft style spaces with royal aesthetics. For summers, the venues would be perfect due to airy indoors but the essence of flowers would be seen covering the pillars, table centers, and big size pots in the corners of the banquet hall.

For night parties, one thing which will be very frequent to capture along with heavy lighting are the dark yet colorful flowers which will be depicting the moods. Though the earlier trends were only limited to light colored flowers, this year would be a different hit with dark flowers combined with textured linens and overall translucent interiors.

Also, for the spring season, the wedding flowers would no longer be restricted to roses or small petal flowers. Rather this year would show up with beautiful and wild lushes with overgrown flowers like dogwood, dahlias, and lavender.
